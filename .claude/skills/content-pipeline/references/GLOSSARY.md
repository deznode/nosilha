# Cape Verdean Cultural Terms Glossary

Reference these terms when authoring or verifying cultural heritage content.

## Core Terms

| Term | Pronunciation | Meaning | Usage Context |
|------|--------------|---------|---------------|
| Morabeza | moh-rah-BEH-zah | Cape Verdean spirit of hospitality, warmth, and genuine welcome | Community atmosphere, business culture, interpersonal interactions |
| Sodade | soh-DAH-deh | Deep longing and nostalgia connecting diaspora to homeland | Emotional bonds, migration stories, heritage connections |
| Cachupa | kah-SHOO-pah | National dish combining corn, beans, and proteins; cultural cornerstone | Food content, celebrations, family traditions |
| Morna | MOR-nah | Traditional music genre expressing sodade and deep emotion | Music heritage, cultural events, artistic traditions |
| Kriolu | kree-OH-loo | Cape Verdean Creole language; primary spoken language of Cape Verde | Language references, authentic dialogue, community voice |
| Grogue | GROH-geh | Cape Verdean sugarcane spirit; social and cultural significance | Food/drink content, social customs, celebrations |
| Tabanka | tah-BAHN-kah | Traditional festival with African roots celebrating community bonds | Cultural events, heritage festivals, community traditions |
| Batuku | bah-TOO-koo | Traditional women's music and dance; Santiago Island origin | Music/dance content, women's cultural roles |
| Funana | foo-NAH-nah | Accordion-driven dance music; originally associated with rural communities | Music heritage, dance traditions, cultural celebrations |
| Coladeira | koh-lah-DAY-rah | Upbeat dance music counterpart to morna | Music heritage, celebrations, contemporary culture |

## Integration Guidelines

1. Integrate terms naturally within cultural narrative — never force them
2. Provide brief explanations for international audiences on first use
3. Use italics for non-English terms on first occurrence
4. Include pronunciation guides when helpful for the audience
5. Connect terms to lived community experiences, not abstract definitions
6. Let community voice define meaning through examples and stories

## Cultural Sensitivity

- Present terms with respect and proper cultural context
- Avoid overuse that feels performative or tokenizing
- Let community members define their own terminology through direct quotes
- Reference `docs/10-product/cultural-heritage-verification.md` for accuracy standards
- Sacred or ceremonial terms may require community permission before publication
