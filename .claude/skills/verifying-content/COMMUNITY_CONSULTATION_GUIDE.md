# Community Consultation Guide

This document provides protocols for engaging with elders, cultural practitioners, and diaspora communities for authentic cultural validation of Cape Verdean heritage content.

## Elder Engagement Protocols

### Initial Contact Procedures

Follow these steps to respectfully approach community elders:

1. **Community Organization Introduction**
   - Contact Brava Cultural Association or Nova Sintra Cultural Center
   - Request formal introduction to relevant elders
   - Explain verification purpose and community benefit clearly
   - Provide written overview of consultation topic in Portuguese/Kriolu

2. **Elder Identification and Selection**
   - Seek recommendations from cultural organizations
   - Prioritize elders with recognized cultural authority
   - Consider geographic diversity (Nova Sintra, Fajã d'Água, Nossa Senhora do Monte)
   - Include both men and women for balanced perspective

3. **Scheduling and Logistics**
   - Schedule at elder convenience (avoid meal times, hot afternoons)
   - Allow flexible timing for elder comfort
   - Offer home visit or cultural center location as preferred
   - Plan for 1-2 hour consultation duration

### Consultation Best Practices

To ensure respectful and productive elder consultations:

**Cultural Protocol Observance**:
- Greet with traditional Cape Verdean respect (handshake, title use)
- Begin with social conversation before cultural topics
- Accept offered refreshments (coffee, grogue) as cultural courtesy
- Follow elder lead on language preference (Portuguese, Kriolu, or mixed)

**Respectful Inquiry Techniques**:
- Ask open-ended questions allowing narrative response
- Avoid leading questions that suggest desired answers
- Show genuine interest in elder knowledge and experience
- Allow time for reflection and memory processing

**Time and Energy Respect**:
- Monitor elder energy levels and offer breaks
- Do not rush consultation for convenience
- Return for additional sessions if needed rather than overextending single visit
- Conclude consultation at first signs of elder fatigue

### Fair Compensation Standards

Provide appropriate compensation for elder time and cultural expertise:

**Monetary Compensation**:
- Minimum: 2,000 CVE ($20 USD) for 1-2 hour consultation
- Extended consultations: 3,000-5,000 CVE ($30-50 USD)
- Multiple session consultations: Negotiate fair total compensation
- Payment in cash, Cape Verdean Escudos, at consultation conclusion

**Alternative Compensation**:
- Grocery items or household goods if preferred by elder
- Transportation assistance for elder or family members
- Copies of final verification report and published content
- Public acknowledgment in verification documentation

**Community Benefit Contributions**:
- Donation to local cultural organization in elder's name
- Support for community cultural events or heritage projects
- Educational materials for local schools or cultural centers
- Long-term relationship building for ongoing consultation

### Documentation and Consent

Obtain proper consent and documentation for elder consultations:

**Recording Consent**:
- Request permission before audio or video recording
- Explain how recordings will be used and stored
- Offer option for notes-only documentation if recording declined
- Respect elder preference for private vs. public knowledge sharing

**Quotation and Attribution**:
- Request explicit permission to quote elder directly
- Confirm preferred name/title for attribution
- Clarify any statements elder prefers off-record
- Provide draft content to elder for review before publication

**Sensitive Knowledge Protection**:
- Identify information elder considers culturally sensitive
- Respect restrictions on sacred knowledge or private traditions
- Implement appropriate access controls as requested
- Obtain community approval for any borderline content

## Cultural Practitioner Coordination

### Practitioner Identification

Locate and engage active tradition keepers through these channels:

**Cultural Organizations**:
- Brava Cultural Association: Traditional music and dance practitioners
- Nova Sintra Cultural Center: Artists, writers, cultural educators
- Fishing Cooperative of Brava: Traditional fishing knowledge keepers
- Women's Collectives: Traditional food preservation, textile practices

**Community Networks**:
- Church organizations: Religious tradition practitioners
- Family networks: Multigenerational tradition keepers
- Diaspora organizations: Cultural practice preservation abroad
- Local businesses: Traditional craft practitioners, restaurateurs

**Direct Outreach**:
- Attend community cultural events and festivals
- Visit traditional practice locations (fishing ports, markets)
- Connect through family referrals and community recommendations
- Build relationships through repeated community presence

### Practice Context Understanding

To authenticate cultural practices effectively:

**Current Practice Documentation**:
- Observe practice in authentic context (not staged demonstration)
- Document contemporary practice details and variations
- Understand economic importance and sustainability
- Identify traditional knowledge transmission methods

**Historical Continuity Validation**:
- Confirm practice longevity through elder testimony
- Identify changes and adaptations over time
- Understand reasons for practice evolution
- Distinguish core tradition from adaptive variation

**Regional Specificity Recording**:
- Document Brava Island-specific practice details
- Identify variations from other Cape Verdean islands
- Understand local environmental and cultural influences
- Record unique terminology and local knowledge

### Economic Considerations

Ensure consultation respects practitioner livelihood:

**Time Compensation**:
- Pay for practitioner time at or above normal work rate
- Compensate for demonstration or instruction time
- Reimburse any materials used in demonstration
- Avoid scheduling during peak work periods

**Knowledge Sharing Terms**:
- Clarify how practitioner knowledge will be credited
- Discuss potential economic benefit from heritage tourism
- Ensure community benefit from cultural content publication
- Protect practitioner intellectual property rights

**Sustainable Relationships**:
- Build ongoing relationships beyond single consultation
- Support practitioner businesses through platform promotion
- Connect practitioners with diaspora customers and supporters
- Advocate for cultural practice preservation and support

- Connect through social media diaspora community groups
- Build relationships through consistent engagement

**Validation Contributions**:
- Diaspora community memory and family history
- Emigration context and homeland connection perspective
- Cultural practice preservation in diaspora settings
- Heritage content authenticity assessment from diaspora perspective

### Virtual Consultation Methods

Conduct remote consultations with diaspora community:

**Technology Platforms**:
- WhatsApp video calls (widely used by Cape Verdean diaspora)
- Zoom/Google Meet for group consultations
- Email exchange for written testimony and documentation
- Social media messaging for ongoing communication

**Virtual Consultation Best Practices**:
- Test technology in advance and provide technical support
- Schedule across time zones considering diaspora location
- Record sessions with explicit permission
- Follow same compensation protocols as in-person consultations

**Documentation Collection**:
- Request family photographs and documents via secure sharing
- Collect scanned historical materials and correspondence
- Gather diaspora community organization records
- Archive oral history recordings with proper metadata

## Community Validation Workflow

### Verification Report Review

Obtain community approval before content publication:

1. **Draft Report Circulation**
   - Share verification report draft with consulted elders and practitioners
   - Allow 2-3 weeks for community review and feedback
   - Provide Portuguese/Kriolu translation if needed
   - Accept written, verbal, or recorded feedback

2. **Community Feedback Integration**
   - Incorporate all factual corrections from community review
   - Address cultural sensitivity concerns immediately
   - Revise framing or emphasis based on community perspective
   - Clarify any misunderstandings or misrepresentations

3. **Final Community Approval**
   - Obtain explicit approval from primary cultural authorities
   - Confirm no outstanding concerns from community reviewers
   - Document community approval in verification report
   - Acknowledge all community contributors appropriately

### Ongoing Community Relationships

Build sustainable community consultation relationships:

**Annual Review Process**:
- Schedule yearly verification report review with community
- Update content based on new community input or corrections
- Monitor community feedback on published heritage content
- Maintain active relationships with cultural authorities

**Community Education Partnership**:
- Share verification reports with local schools and cultural centers
- Support community cultural education initiatives
- Provide research resources to community organizations
- Collaborate on heritage preservation projects

**Reciprocal Support**:
- Promote community cultural events and initiatives
- Support local cultural practitioners and organizations
- Advocate for community cultural preservation priorities
- Ensure platform serves ongoing community benefit

## Cultural Sensitivity Guidelines

### Sacred Knowledge Protection

Respect cultural intellectual property and sensitive information:

**Sacred Practice Identification**:
- Ask explicitly about culturally sensitive or sacred knowledge
- Respect elder or practitioner restrictions on information sharing
- Identify practices requiring community permission for publication
- Implement appropriate access controls for sensitive content

**Community Privacy Maintenance**:
- Protect personal information and family privacy
- Avoid publishing sensitive community economic information
- Respect requests for anonymity or limited attribution
- Consult community about potentially sensitive content

**Cultural Intellectual Property**:
- Acknowledge community ownership of traditional knowledge
- Ensure economic benefit flows to community for heritage tourism
- Protect against cultural appropriation or exploitation
- Advocate for community cultural rights and recognition

### Respectful Representation Standards

Ensure all verification work serves community interests:

**Community Benefit Priority**:
- Verify content serves cultural preservation and community pride
- Avoid content that could harm community reputation or interests
- Ensure economic opportunities flow to local community
- Support diaspora homeland connection and cultural identity

**Authentic Voice Preservation**:
- Prioritize direct community quotes and perspectives
- Avoid external observer interpretation or framing
- Present community as sophisticated cultural experts
- Respect community authority in cultural matters

**Cultural Complexity Recognition**:
- Avoid oversimplification of complex cultural practices
- Acknowledge diversity within Cape Verdean community
- Present living, evolving culture rather than frozen tradition
- Connect contemporary practice to historical continuity

## Consultation Documentation Standards

### Contact Information Recording

Maintain comprehensive community consultation records:

**Community Contact Database**:
- Name, title, contact information (phone, email, address)
- Cultural expertise area and community role
- Consultation dates and topics discussed
- Compensation provided and consent obtained
- Follow-up communication and ongoing relationship status

**Privacy and Security**:
- Store contact information securely
- Obtain permission before sharing contact details
- Protect community member privacy
- Maintain confidentiality for sensitive consultations

### Consultation Session Documentation

Record detailed consultation information:

**Session Metadata**:
- Date, time, location of consultation
- Participants (community members, verification team)
- Duration and language(s) used
- Recording method (audio, video, notes only)
- Compensation provided

**Content Documentation**:
- Key testimony and community knowledge shared
- Historical information and cultural practice details
- Community perspectives and framing guidance
- Sensitive information restrictions or access controls
- Follow-up questions or additional consultation needs

**Evidence Trail**:
- Verification report references to consultation
- Community approval documentation
- Attribution and quotation permissions
- Ongoing monitoring and review schedule

- [ ] Elder/practitioner comfort and energy monitored

### Post-Consultation
- [ ] Thank you communication sent
- [ ] Consultation notes transcribed and organized
- [ ] Evidence trail documented in verification system
- [ ] Draft verification report prepared
- [ ] Community review copy provided

**Remember**: Community consultation is not a one-time transaction but an ongoing relationship built on mutual respect, fair compensation, and shared commitment to cultural preservation. Every consultation strengthens the platform's connection to authentic Cape Verdean heritage and community authority.
