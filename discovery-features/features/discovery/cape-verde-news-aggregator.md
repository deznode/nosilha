# Discovery: Cape Verde News Aggregator Platform

**Status:** Discovery Phase  
**Priority:** High - Critical for diaspora and local community information needs  
**Created:** 2025-08-22  
**Discovery Owner:** Community Content Team  
**Target Completion:** 4-5 weeks for comprehensive community validation and technical research

## 🔍 Community Problem Investigation

### Observed Community Need:
> **"Cape Verdean diaspora and local communities lack a centralized, reliable source for staying informed about Cape Verde news, diaspora community events, and cultural developments worldwide. Current information is scattered across multiple sources in different languages, making it difficult to maintain connection with homeland and global community developments."**

### Problem Evidence:
- **Information Fragmentation:** News about Cape Verde scattered across Portuguese, English, French sources
- **Diaspora Disconnect:** Global Cape Verdean communities miss important homeland developments
- **Local Isolation:** Brava Island residents lack easy access to broader Cape Verde and diaspora news
- **Cultural Events:** Community events, cultural celebrations, achievements often go unnoticed outside local areas
- **Language Barriers:** Some community members struggle with Portuguese-only or English-only sources

### User Groups Affected:
- **Primary:** Cape Verdean diaspora in US, Portugal, France, Netherlands seeking homeland connection
- **Secondary:** Local Cape Verde residents wanting broader national and diaspora community awareness
- **Stakeholders:** Community organizations, cultural groups, local media, government communications

## 📝 Discovery Research Plan

### Research Questions:
1. **Information Sources:** What Cape Verde news sources exist and how reliable/accessible are they?
2. **Community Preferences:** How do diaspora and local communities currently stay informed?
3. **Language Needs:** What language combinations are needed for different community segments?
4. **Content Categories:** What types of news/information are most valuable to different user groups?
5. **Platform Features:** What community-driven features would enhance information sharing?

### Research Methods:
- [ ] **Cape Verde Media Analysis:** Comprehensive analysis of existing news sources
  - Target: Map 15-20 major Cape Verde news sources (print, online, radio, TV)
  - Focus: Content quality, update frequency, language, accessibility, bias assessment
  - Timeline: Week 1-2 - Complete media landscape analysis

- [ ] **Diaspora Community Interviews:** How communities currently stay informed
  - Participants: 10+ diaspora community leaders across US, Portugal, France, Netherlands
  - Format: Video interviews about information needs, current sources, platform preferences
  - Timeline: Week 2-3 - Conduct diaspora information needs assessment

- [ ] **Local Community Validation:** Cape Verde resident information consumption patterns
  - Participants: Local residents across different islands, age groups, education levels
  - Method: Surveys and focus groups about news consumption and information needs
  - Timeline: Week 3-4 - Local community information patterns research

- [ ] **Technical Feasibility Research:** Aggregation and content management capabilities
  - Focus: RSS feeds, web scraping, content licensing, multilingual processing
  - Analysis: Technical requirements for real-time aggregation and cultural context
  - Timeline: Week 4-5 - Technical architecture and content licensing research

### Discovery Timeline:
- **Week 1:** Cape Verde media landscape mapping and source analysis
- **Week 2:** Diaspora community interviews and information needs assessment
- **Week 3:** Local community validation and content preference research
- **Week 4:** Technical feasibility and content licensing investigation

## 🚀 Go/No-Go Decision Framework

### Criteria for Moving to Feature Development:
- [ ] **Community Need Confirmed:** Clear evidence diaspora and local communities want unified news platform
- [ ] **Content Sources Identified:** Reliable Cape Verde news sources available for aggregation
- [ ] **Technical Feasibility:** Platform architecture viable for real-time multilingual news aggregation
- [ ] **Community Support:** Diaspora organizations and local communities endorse platform concept
- [ ] **Content Licensing:** Legal framework established for news source aggregation

### Risk Assessment:
- **High Risk:** Content licensing and copyright issues with news source aggregation
- **Medium Risk:** Technical complexity of multilingual, real-time content processing
- **Medium Risk:** Maintaining content quality and preventing misinformation spread
- **Low Risk:** Community interest and engagement (strong diaspora information needs)

### Resource Requirements (if proceeding):
- **Content Management:** Ongoing curation, translation, cultural context addition
- **Technical Infrastructure:** Real-time aggregation, content processing, mobile optimization
- **Community Engagement:** Partnerships with news sources and diaspora organizations
- **Multilingual Support:** Translation capabilities and cultural context for different communities
