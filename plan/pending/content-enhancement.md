# LLM Research Prompts for Nos Ilha Content

This document provides comprehensive prompts for LLM-based research to enrich each page and section of the Nos Ilha platform with deep historical, cultural, and contextual content about Brava Island, Cape Verde.

**Important Context**: Brava is the smallest inhabited island in Cape Verde with approximately 6,000 current residents. Due to its size and relative isolation, comprehensive information may be limited online. These prompts are designed to work with available sources while acknowledging information gaps and emphasizing community knowledge, oral history, and diaspora connections.

## Introduction & Guidelines

### Purpose
These prompts are designed to generate rich, culturally-authentic content for specific pages and sections of the Nos Ilha website (nosilha.com), ensuring each area has specialized research guidance while avoiding content duplication across the platform.

### Website-Focused Research Strategy
This document organizes research prompts by **specific website pages** to ensure:
- **Specialized Content**: Each prompt targets unique content for specific pages
- **No Duplication**: Research areas don't overlap between different website sections
- **Visitor-Focused**: Content serves both educational and practical purposes for site visitors
- **Community-Centered**: Authentic local voices and diaspora perspectives throughout
- **Contemporary Relevance**: Balance of historical heritage with current realities

### How to Use These Prompts
1. **Select by Website Page**: Choose the prompt that corresponds to the specific page you're researching content for
2. **Focus on Unique Content**: Each prompt specializes in content unique to that page/section
3. **Maintain Consistency**: Use consistent voice and cultural sensitivity across all research
4. **Verify & Community Review**: Prioritize community validation of all content
5. **Update Regularly**: Plan for ongoing content updates as community relationships develop

### Research Principles
- **Authenticity**: Prioritize local voices and perspectives
- **Accuracy**: Cross-reference information when possible, acknowledge when sources are limited
- **Cultural Sensitivity**: Respect Cape Verdean culture and traditions
- **Diaspora Inclusion**: Consider the global Cape Verdean community as key knowledge holders
- **Contemporary Relevance**: Connect historical content to present day
- **Community-Centered**: Focus on living memory and personal stories from the small island community
- **Quality over Quantity**: Better to have authentic, limited information than extensive but unreliable content

### People Page Research

#### Historical Figures Research Prompt (Enhanced)

```text
You are researching notable figures from Brava Island for a cultural heritage platform using a chronological, influence-based approach that reveals the island's extraordinary impact on Cape Verdean culture and global diaspora communities.

**Research Context**: Brava is a small island (~6,000 residents) with limited formal biographical documentation but an outsized cultural influence. This research framework organizes figures chronologically by historical eras, assesses their relative influence, and connects them to broader historical movements. Focus on authenticated figures while acknowledging documentation limitations.

## CHRONOLOGICAL RESEARCH FRAMEWORK

### **Era 1: Cultural Foundation (1867-1930)**
**Historical Context**: Birth of Cape Verdean cultural identity through literature and music
**Key Characteristics**: 
- Emergence of distinct cultural voice through morna and literature
- Pioneer use of Crioulo as literary language
- Foundation of emotional and artistic expressions defining national identity

**Research Priorities for This Era:**
- Literary pioneers (Eugénio Tavares as primary example)
- Early musical masters and instrument makers
- Cultural innovators who transformed traditional forms
- Figures who elevated vernacular culture to national significance

### **Era 2: Political Awakening (1900-1975)**
**Historical Context**: Translation of cultural pride into political action and national liberation
**Key Characteristics**:
- Direct participation in independence movements
- Connection to anti-colonial struggles
- Bridge between cultural identity and political consciousness

**Research Priorities for This Era:**
- Independence movement participants and collaborators
- Intellectuals who connected culture to politics
- Religious pioneers and social reformers
- Community leaders during colonial resistance

### **Era 3: Diaspora Expansion (1880s-1960s)**
**Historical Context**: Global influence through migration and institution-building
**Key Characteristics**:
- Extraordinary international achievements despite small island origins
- Creation of lasting religious, cultural, and economic institutions
- Preservation of cultural traditions across continents

**Research Priorities for This Era:**
- Religious leaders and institution founders
- Cultural preservationists in diaspora communities
- Economic and social pioneers
- Bridge-builders between homeland and new countries

### **Era 4: Contemporary Era (1975-Present)**
**Historical Context**: Modern leadership and cultural preservation in the global age
**Key Characteristics**:
- Continued tradition of disproportionate global influence
- Active cultural preservation and revitalization efforts
- Modern professional and political leadership
- Digital age cultural transmission

**Research Priorities for This Era:**
- Political and legal leaders with national/international influence
- Contemporary cultural preservationists and artists
- Modern institution builders and community organizers
- Technology-enabled cultural transmission leaders

## INFLUENCE ASSESSMENT FRAMEWORK

Evaluate each figure's impact using these categories:

**Revolutionary Impact**: Figures who fundamentally transformed cultural, political, or social landscapes
- *Example: Eugénio Tavares (transformed morna, established Crioulo literature)*
- *Criteria: Created lasting change, influenced entire movements, recognized nationally/internationally*

**Phenomenal Impact**: Figures who achieved extraordinary success and created major institutions
- *Example: Marcelino "Daddy" Grace (built religious empire with millions of followers)*
- *Criteria: Built major institutions, achieved exceptional recognition, influenced large populations*

**National Impact**: Figures who achieved significant recognition in national contexts
- *Example: David Soares (prominent US district attorney with national reputation)*
- *Criteria: National recognition, policy influence, significant professional achievement*

**Global Impact**: Figures whose influence spread across multiple countries or continents
- *Example: João José Dias (founded religious movement across 44 African nations)*
- *Criteria: International influence, cross-border impact, global institution building*

**Cultural Impact**: Figures who significantly preserved, transmitted, or evolved cultural traditions
- *Example: Roosevelt Pires (continues family luthier tradition, preserves craftsmanship)*
- *Criteria: Cultural preservation, artistic achievement, tradition transmission*

**Community Impact**: Figures who profoundly influenced local or regional communities
- *Example: Padre Pio Gottin (served Brava community for 24 years, fed children during famines)*
- *Criteria: Local leadership, community service, grassroots influence*

**Religious Leadership**: Multi-generational influence through spiritual institutions

## QUALITY ASSESSMENT CRITERIA

### **Source Reliability Hierarchy:**
1. **Primary Sources**: Official documents, personal writings, contemporary records
2. **Secondary Academic Sources**: Scholarly research, historical analyses
3. **Community Documentation**: Diaspora community records, cultural organization materials
4. **Oral Tradition**: Community memory, family stories, cultural knowledge
5. **Community Memory**: Living memory from current residents and diaspora

### **Biographical Completeness Standards:**
- **Complete Profile**: Birth/death dates, major achievements, documented influence, cultural context
- **Partial Profile**: Some biographical details, documented achievements, unclear influence scope
- **Community Memory Profile**: Limited documentation, strong community knowledge, unclear dates
- **Research Needed**: Minimal information, requires additional investigation

- Can specific achievements be documented?
- Is the geographic/temporal scope of influence clear?
- Are there measurable outcomes or lasting institutions?
- How do contemporary sources assess their importance?
- What is the quality and reliability of influence claims?
```

### Verification and Accuracy

#### Source Evaluation Criteria (Adapted for Limited Information Context)
- **Community Voices**: Prioritize perspectives from Brava residents and diaspora communities (most reliable source)
- **Primary Sources**: Use available first-hand accounts, historical documents, and direct testimonies
- **Academic Sources**: Use scholarly research on Cape Verdean history and culture that includes Brava
- **Cross-Referencing**: Verify information when multiple sources exist; acknowledge when single-source
- **Cultural Sensitivity**: Ensure respectful treatment of cultural practices and beliefs
- **Transparency**: Clearly indicate the strength and type of sources for all information

#### Information Validation Process (Adapted for Small Island Context)
1. **Available Source Confirmation**: Seek confirmation when multiple sources exist; acknowledge when only one source available
2. **Community Review**: Prioritize having local community members review content for accuracy
3. **Cultural Context**: Ensure information is presented within appropriate cultural context
4. **Contemporary Relevance**: Verify that historical information accurately represents current understanding
5. **Bias Awareness**: Acknowledge potential biases in sources and present balanced perspectives
6. **Information Gaps**: Clearly indicate when information is incomplete, uncertain, or unavailable
7. **Living Memory vs Documentation**: Distinguish between formally documented information and community memory

### Cultural Sensitivity Guidelines

#### Respectful Research Practices
- **Community Ownership**: Recognize that cultural knowledge belongs to the community
- **Collaborative Approach**: Involve community members in research and verification processes
- **Appropriate Attribution**: Credit sources and respect intellectual property
- **Sensitive Topics**: Handle topics like emigration, poverty, and social challenges with care
- **Contemporary Relevance**: Connect historical information to contemporary community life

- **Authentic Voice**: Present information in a way that honors the community's perspective
- **Balanced Perspective**: Include multiple viewpoints and avoid oversimplification
- **Living Culture**: Present culture as dynamic and evolving, not static or historical only
- **Community Agency**: Emphasize community members as active agents in their cultural preservation
- **Respectful Language**: Use terminology that is respectful and culturally appropriate
