# LLM Research Prompts for Nos Ilha Content

This document provides comprehensive prompts for LLM-based research to enrich each page and section of the Nos Ilha platform with deep historical, cultural, and contextual content about Brava Island, Cape Verde.

**Important Context**: Brava is the smallest inhabited island in Cape Verde with approximately 6,000 current residents. Due to its size and relative isolation, comprehensive information may be limited online. These prompts are designed to work with available sources while acknowledging information gaps and emphasizing community knowledge, oral history, and diaspora connections.

## Introduction & Guidelines

### Purpose
These prompts are designed to generate rich, culturally-authentic content that honors Brava Island's heritage while providing valuable information to visitors and the diaspora community.

### How to Use These Prompts
1. **Select the appropriate prompt** for the content area you're researching
2. **Customize the prompt** with specific details (dates, names, locations) as needed
3. **Combine multiple prompts** for comprehensive coverage
4. **Verify information** through multiple sources when possible
5. **Maintain cultural sensitivity** and respect for local perspectives
6. **Acknowledge limitations** when information is scarce or unavailable
7. **Prioritize community voices** and living memory over extensive historical documentation

### Research Principles
- **Authenticity**: Prioritize local voices and perspectives
- **Accuracy**: Cross-reference information when possible, acknowledge when sources are limited
- **Cultural Sensitivity**: Respect Cape Verdean culture and traditions
- **Diaspora Inclusion**: Consider the global Cape Verdean community as key knowledge holders
- **Contemporary Relevance**: Connect historical content to present day
- **Community-Centered**: Focus on living memory and personal stories from the small island community
- **Quality over Quantity**: Better to have authentic, limited information than extensive but unreliable content

## Core Content Pages

### History Page Research

#### Comprehensive Historical Research Prompt

```text
You are a cultural historian specializing in Cape Verdean history. Research and provide detailed information about Brava Island's history for a community heritage website. 

**Research Context**: Brava is the smallest inhabited island in Cape Verde (~6,000 residents). Historical documentation may be limited compared to larger islands. Focus on available sources and acknowledge information gaps. Prioritize community knowledge and diaspora sources when formal historical records are sparse.

Focus on:

**Pre-Colonial Period (Before 1462)**
- Geological formation of Brava Island through volcanic activity
- Any evidence of pre-Portuguese contact or settlement
- Natural environment and ecosystems before human settlement

**Discovery and Early Colonial Period (1462-1680s)**
- Portuguese discovery in 1462 and early exploration
- Reasons why Brava remained largely uninhabited initially
- Comparison with settlement patterns on other Cape Verde islands

**Settlement Period (1680s-1800s)**
- Migration from Fogo Island following volcanic eruptions
- Early settler communities and their origins
- Development of agriculture and early economy
- Social structures and community organization

**Cultural Flowering (1800s-1900s)**
- Emergence of Brava as a cultural center
- Development of musical and literary traditions
- Key cultural figures and their contributions
- Connection to Cape Verdean identity formation

**Modern Era (1900s-Present)**
- Impact of independence in 1975
- Emigration patterns and diaspora development
- Contemporary challenges and opportunities
- Cultural preservation efforts

**Research Questions to Address:**
1. What specific events led to Brava becoming known as the "Island of Flowers" and "Island of Music"?
2. How did the island's geography influence its cultural development?
3. What role did Brava play in Cape Verde's independence movement?
4. How have emigration patterns shaped the island's development?
5. What are the most significant cultural contributions Brava has made to Cape Verde?

**Sources to Consider (in order of likely availability):**
- Diaspora community records, especially from New England Cape Verdean communities
- Oral histories from current and former Brava residents
- Cape Verdean literary and musical archives (Eugénio Tavares and other Brava figures)
- Academic research on Cape Verde that includes Brava
- Portuguese colonial records (limited for smaller islands)
- Family genealogies and personal collections

**Content Guidelines:**
- Provide detailed narrative content where sources allow
- Clearly indicate when information is limited or uncertain
- Include "What we know" vs "What community members remember" vs "What we'd like to learn more about"
- Focus on the most well-documented periods and events
- Acknowledge that some historical details may be incomplete due to the island's size and limited formal documentation
```

```text

Provide comprehensive biographical information and cultural context for both historical and contemporary figures who have contributed to Brava Island's cultural legacy.

**Research Priorities (focus on the most documented):**

**Primary Focus - Well-Documented Figures**
- Eugénio Tavares (1867-1930): extensive documentation available
- Other nationally recognized figures with sufficient biographical material
- Focus on 3-5 key historical figures rather than attempting comprehensive coverage

**Secondary Focus - Community Memory**
- Local musicians, storytellers, and community leaders remembered by residents
- Figures important to island life but with limited formal documentation
- Contemporary cultural preservationists and community contributors

**Musical Heritage**
- Traditional musicians who preserved Brava's musical heritage
- Composers and performers of morna and coladeira
- Instrument makers and music teachers
- Contemporary musicians maintaining traditions

**Community Leaders**
- Traditional leaders and their roles in community organization
- Religious figures and their cultural influence
- Women leaders and their contributions to cultural preservation
- Contemporary community organizers and cultural advocates

**Artisans and Craftspeople**
- Traditional craftsmen: weavers, carpenters, stoneworkers
- Their techniques and cultural significance
- How crafts reflected daily life and cultural values
- Contemporary artisans maintaining traditional skills

**Diaspora Contributors**
- Brava natives who achieved recognition internationally
- Their efforts to maintain connections with the island
- Contributions to preserving and promoting Brava culture abroad
- Second and third-generation diaspora members contributing to heritage

**Research Questions:**
1. What personal stories and anecdotes reveal the character of these figures?
2. How did their work reflect broader Cape Verdean cultural themes?
3. What specific works, achievements, or contributions are they known for?
4. How are they remembered and honored today?
5. What influence did they have on younger generations?
6. How did their connection to Brava shape their broader work?

**Content Approach:**
- **Comprehensive profiles** for well-documented figures (Eugénio Tavares, etc.)
- **Brief profiles** for figures with limited documentation
- **Community memory sections** for locally important but undocumented figures
- **"Stories we've heard"** sections for figures known primarily through oral tradition

**For Each Figure, Include (when available):**
- Birth and death dates, family background
- Major achievements and contributions
- Specific works, compositions, or accomplishments
- Personal stories and characteristics that community members remember
- Legacy and continuing influence
- **Clearly indicate** when information is based on community memory vs documented sources

**Research Approach:**
- Start with the most well-documented figures
- Acknowledge when biographical details are incomplete
- Include community stories and memories even when formal documentation is limited
- Focus on cultural significance rather than exhaustive biographical detail
```

## Research Quality Guidelines

### Verification and Accuracy

#### Source Evaluation Criteria (Adapted for Limited Information Context)
- **Community Voices**: Prioritize perspectives from Brava residents and diaspora communities (most reliable source)
- **Primary Sources**: Use available first-hand accounts, historical documents, and direct testimonies
- **Academic Sources**: Use scholarly research on Cape Verdean history and culture that includes Brava
- **Cross-Referencing**: Verify information when multiple sources exist; acknowledge when single-source
- **Cultural Sensitivity**: Ensure respectful treatment of cultural practices and beliefs
- **Transparency**: Clearly indicate the strength and type of sources for all information

#### Information Validation Process (Adapted for Small Island Context)
1. **Available Source Confirmation**: Seek confirmation when multiple sources exist; acknowledge when only one source available
2. **Community Review**: Prioritize having local community members review content for accuracy
3. **Cultural Context**: Ensure information is presented within appropriate cultural context
4. **Contemporary Relevance**: Verify that historical information accurately represents current understanding
5. **Bias Awareness**: Acknowledge potential biases in sources and present balanced perspectives
6. **Information Gaps**: Clearly indicate when information is incomplete, uncertain, or unavailable
7. **Living Memory vs Documentation**: Distinguish between formally documented information and community memory

### Cultural Sensitivity Guidelines

#### Respectful Research Practices
- **Community Ownership**: Recognize that cultural knowledge belongs to the community
- **Collaborative Approach**: Involve community members in research and verification processes
- **Appropriate Attribution**: Credit sources and respect intellectual property
- **Sensitive Topics**: Handle topics like emigration, poverty, and social challenges with care
- **Contemporary Relevance**: Connect historical information to contemporary community life

#### Content Presentation Standards
- **Authentic Voice**: Present information in a way that honors the community's perspective
- **Balanced Perspective**: Include multiple viewpoints and avoid oversimplification
- **Living Culture**: Present culture as dynamic and evolving, not static or historical only
- **Community Agency**: Emphasize community members as active agents in their cultural preservation
- **Respectful Language**: Use terminology that is respectful and culturally appropriate

### Implementation Notes

#### Content Integration for Small Island Context
- Adapt research findings to fit website structure while acknowledging information limitations
- Create content that serves both educational and practical purposes for visitors and diaspora
- Ensure accessibility for diverse audiences including diaspora communities who may be primary knowledge holders
- Maintain consistency in tone and approach across different content areas
- Include clear indicators when information is limited or based on community memory
- Design content structure to accommodate future additions as more information becomes available

#### Ongoing Research Strategy
- **Prioritize community collaboration** as the primary source of authentic information
- Create processes for community members to contribute stories, corrections, and additional information
- Establish connections with Cape Verdean diaspora communities, especially in New England
- Plan for regular content updates and expansions as relationships with community develop
- Document sources and maintain research archives for future reference
- Encourage community storytelling and oral history collection through the platform
- **Accept that some areas will have limited information** and focus on quality over quantity

#### Working with Limited Information
- **Start with what's available** rather than waiting for comprehensive coverage
- **Build incrementally** as community relationships develop and more information becomes available
- **Celebrate authentic stories** even when formal documentation is limited
- **Create space for community contributions** and ongoing information sharing
- **Acknowledge limitations transparently** while highlighting the richness of available content
