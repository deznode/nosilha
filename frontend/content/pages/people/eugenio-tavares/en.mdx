# Eugénio Tavares (1867-1930)

Eugénio de Paula Tavares, born in Brava on October 18, 1867, transformed Cape Verdean culture through his revolutionary approach to morna music and his pioneering use of Crioulo as a literary language.

## The Poet of Sodade

Tavares elevated the morna from its earlier satirical form into the deeply emotional genre that would become Cape Verde's musical signature. His compositions captured _sodade_—the profound longing that defines the Cape Verdean diaspora experience.

## Literary Pioneer

Beyond music, Tavares was the first to use Crioulo as a serious literary medium, publishing poetry that celebrated the vernacular language and gave voice to Cape Verdean identity.

## Exile and Return

From 1900-1910, Tavares lived in exile in New Bedford, Massachusetts. This period of separation from his beloved Brava deepened his artistic exploration of longing and loss, themes that would permeate his most celebrated works.

## Legacy

Today, Eugénio Tavares is celebrated as Cape Verde's cultural patriarch. His home in Brava has been preserved as a museum, and his compositions remain central to Cape Verdean musical tradition.
