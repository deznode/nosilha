# Marcelino "Daddy" Grace (1881-1960)

Born Marcelino Manuel da Graça on Brava, Cape Verde, he would become one of the most influential religious leaders in American history under the name "Sweet Daddy Grace."

## From Brava to America

Like many Bravenses of his era, young Marcelino followed the whaling routes to New Bedford, Massachusetts. There, he worked various jobs before discovering his calling as a spiritual leader.

## Building an Empire

Starting with just $39 and a tent in Massachusetts, Daddy Grace built the United House of Prayer for All People into a denomination with over 3 million followers and assets worth $25 million at the time of his death.

## Flamboyant Faith

Known for his colorful style—long fingernails painted red, white, and blue, elaborate robes, and charismatic preaching—Daddy Grace attracted followers through a mix of Pentecostal fervor and theatrical presentation.

## The Brava Connection

Though he spent most of his life in America, Daddy Grace never forgot his Cape Verdean roots. His success exemplifies the extraordinary achievements of the Brava diaspora and their lasting impact on American religious life.

## Legacy

The United House of Prayer continues today with active congregations across the United States, a testament to the enduring influence of this son of Brava.
